About the WINDI System

Pre-AI Governance Layer for Institutional Workflow Orchestration

Kempten, Bavaria, Germany • Institutional Beta v1.0 • February 2026

WINDI is a governance infrastructure that operates between human decision-makers and AI systems. It ensures that every AI-assisted interaction is transparent, traceable, and aligned with institutional policy—in real time, during the work itself.

The name stands for We Invite New Decision Intelligence. The system was developed as part of a European research initiative examining how artificial intelligence can be aligned with institutional accountability rather than merely technical performance.

WINDI does not replace AI models, nor does it replace human judgment. It occupies the space between them: a governance layer that detects institutional context, applies policy, explains its reasoning, and makes the governance state visible to the human at all times.

What Makes It Different

Most AI governance approaches operate either before or after the moment of human–AI interaction. WINDI operates during that moment.

How It Works

WINDI operates through a six-phase cognitive governance cycle. As a human drafts a document in the A4 Desk BABEL editor, the system continuously:

Perceives institutional references in the text. Interprets their governance implications through policy engines. Decides the appropriate governance level. Explains its reasoning in the user’s language. Manifests the governance state visually in the editor. Documents the process as traceable institutional knowledge.

Every document produced carries a WINDI-RECEIPT: a cryptographic record of the governance process including authorship, governance level, integrity hash, and institutional witness context.
